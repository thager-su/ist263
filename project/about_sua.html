SUA Overview

Syracuse University Ambulance is a student run service under the Syracuse University Health Services, that responds to more than 1500 emergency calls every year. SUA has been operating as a volunteer service since 1973, at which time members responded to emergency calls on foot.

Emergent Transport

SUA responds to more than 1500 emergencies a year, and can provide intermediate life support and rapid cardiac defibrilation and transportation to all of the hospitals in the area. SUA supports the basic and advanced EMT levels of care, and has an agreement to work with American Medical Response through mutual aid for all Advanced Life Support needs. SUA operates two full time Basic Life Support (BLS) stocked ambulances, which are compliant with the NYS Department of Health regulations. These ambulances are ready to respond to emergencies day or night. SUA also operates a BLS stocked supervisor's flycar which supervisors use to provide extra support on scene or to tow the mass casualty incident trailer when needed.

Non-Emergent Transport

Syracuse University also provides a non-emergent Medical Transport Service (MTS). MTS is also based out of Lyman Hall and provides transportation for students to and from hospitals and urgent cares. MTS is also available to students who have injuries or medical complications that prevent them from getting to and from classes on their own. MTS drivers operate 24/7, with limited coverage between 10pm and 7am for students who need transportation to and from emergency rooms and urgent cares. MTS covers all of SU and ESF campus, as well as near campus neighborhoods. If you are interested in becoming an MTS driver please fill out this application.

Standby Services

In addition to providing 24/7 emergency medical response services, SUA also offers standby services for major events and public gatherings. SUA provides the option of staffing a full ambulance or foot responders. Foot responders bring BLS stocked jump bags, which contain all of the medical equipment needed for emergent treatment. For events in the carrier dome, SUA works with Carrier Dome Medical teams in order to ensure participant and spectator safety. If interested in standby services, please fill out this form at least seven business days in advance.
