Background Information

In SUA each member is given an IBM in order to identify themselves over the radio. Members are not allowed to say names over the radio because the channel is public and doing this would violate privacy. Every member's IBM, except for the field supervisors, is a number between 900 and 999. The EMS manager and the field supervisors all use their call sign as their IBM as well. The EMS manager has call sign Car 1, while each of the field supervisors has a Car number between 3 and 7. Each of their IBMs is their car number with a 20 in front of it.

When new members become full members they start as attendants who assist with patient care. Once they have enough experience they can start driving or running calls, if they are an Emergency Medical Technician (EMT). After that, members can keep moving through the organization. Each positions starts as a progression requiring the member to work with a trainer until they are cleared to work on their own. The highest progression is known as Field Training Officer and they are qualified to train anyone. Members can also pursue a dispatch tract. Members who are both cleared Crew Chiefs and Drivers can become first responders and are allowed to respond to calls on their own in S4, the supervisor's first response vehicle.
